**Round thesis.** This round attacks the recurring failure that every prior engine either starves or enters at the move's midpoint, while the self-referential gate keeps winning with nothing worthy behind it. The unifying move is INVERSION and EARLIER-TIMING: where a lens died on a fade, we propose its continuation inverse (round-number breach not snap; one-sided expansion not absorption); where imports died entering on the visible impulse (Omori, R0), we time the branching-birth loading phase before displacement; where sign-entropy had no headroom, we switch to magnitude-compressibility which volatility-clustering guarantees has headroom; and critically, we honor the standing prescription by pairing the proven regime gate with a genuine momentum-persistence engine firing >=1/sym/day so the armed sample finally reaches decidability. ## 1. `trend_persistence_regime_gated_engine`  ·  lens: gate_engine_pairing

**Mechanism.** The self-referential gate says 'random entries are currently failing = the market is directional, not chopping.' The engine most amplified by that condition is a strong-trend-continuation initiation: a symbol that has closed above its own 24-bar high on rising realized volatility is in a directional impulse, and when the gate confirms that the ambient regime is punishing mean-reverters (random-arm win rate low, stop-out clustering high), the continuation has structural tailwind rather than fading into chop. Why not arbitraged at 5-min retail: the edge is not in the breakout itself (that died as ORB/macross) — it is in the CONDITIONING. Retail cannot compute a running null-arm failure rate across a book of random paper trades; the gate is a private state variable, not a chartable pattern, so the joint distribution of (fresh 24-bar high) x (regime punishing reversion) is not something a retail screen surfaces.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: drawdown_regime_contrarian_gate (r001, best-ever but engine too weak) and macross_family (crossovers die in chop). This is NOT a descendant of macross because the entry does not fire on every crossover into chop — it only fires when the gate certifies the regime is directional (null-arm failing), which is the exact information the prior gate carried but which was wasted on a 24-bar breakout engine. This is the prescribed 'pair the gate with a better engine' successor: the engine is momentum-persistence with a volatility-expansion filter, not a bare Donchian break.

**Entry rule.** Compute system_state via context_keys=['system_state']: over trailing 100 closed paper trades, if null/random-arm win rate < 0.38 AND stop-out clustering index (fraction of last 20 exits that were stops occurring within 3 bars of each other) > 0.5, regime is ARMED. Engine (only fires when ARMED): enter long if close > max(high) of prior 24 bars AND current-bar realized vol (stddev of last 12 bar returns) > 1.3x the median of the prior 96 bars' 12-bar realized vol AND close is in top 20% of current bar range. Symmetric short on 24-bar low with same vol/close-position conditions. One fire per symbol per cooldown.

**Params.** `{"donchian_lookback": 24, "vol_window": 12, "vol_baseline_window": 96, "vol_expansion_mult": 1.3, "close_position_pct": 0.2, "null_winrate_thresh": 0.38, "stopcluster_thresh": 0.5, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 24}`

**Fire rate.** Raw engine base rate: a fresh 24-bar extreme with vol-expansion + strong close occurs empirically ~0.5-1.0/sym/day (288 bars; new 24-bar extremes happen a few times daily, the vol+close filter cuts ~60-70%). Gate is ARMED perhaps 25-35% of calendar time (regime-dependent). Armed fires: ~0.15-0.30/sym/day -> across 5 symbols ~1-1.5/day. Over 930d that is n~1000-1400 raw, ~250-450 armed — clears the n>=100 decidability bar the prescription demands.

**Fee survival.** The armed regime is defined as one where directional moves follow through and reversions get punished — precisely the condition under which a +6% target is reachable before a -3% stop. Unlike the gross-positive traps, the amplitude is not asserted; it is the regime's defining property (null-arm stops clustering = trends running). Vol-expansion filter (1.3x) ensures entries occur in high-range regimes where 6% moves within 24h are common, not in the compressed regime where breaks die under 1%.

**Kill criterion.** Armed expectancy < +0.5%/trade over the full 930d window with n>=100 armed trades. If the better engine ALSO cannot lift armed expectancy above the fee floor at n>=100, the gate's information is not convertible by any continuation engine and the gate family should be retired.

## 2. `return_magnitude_compressibility_break`  ·  lens: information_theory

**Mechanism.** The entropy-of-signs lens died twice because 5-min sign sequences already live at H~0.95/1.0 bits — no headroom. But MAGNITUDE sequences are not maxentropy: absolute returns cluster (volatility clustering is the most robust stylized fact in all of finance). The signal is the compressibility of the recent |return| sequence: when the last N bars' magnitude sequence becomes SUDDENLY LESS compressible (a novel large magnitude arrives that the recent low-vol code cannot cheaply encode), it marks a genuine volatility-regime birth, not noise. This is a directional-agnostic novelty detector on magnitudes, converted to a directional trade by taking the sign of the surprising bar. Not arbitraged: retail volatility screens use realized vol or ATR (level-based), which is slow and symmetric; a compressibility/surprise metric fires on the FIRST bar that breaks the recent magnitude code, capturing the regime birth rather than its confirmed midpoint (the r0/omori chasing failure).

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: entropy_collapse_impulse and conditional_entropy_regime_expansion — both dead because SIGN entropy has no headroom at 5-min. This is explicitly the surviving branch the epitaph named: 'if information theory returns, it must use MAGNITUDES or longer bars, not signs.' This uses magnitudes. It is also NOT the r0/vol-thrust chasing lineage because it fires on the surprise itself with a magnitude threshold that puts entry at the regime birth, and the pre-check below verifies magnitude sequences actually have compressibility headroom (unlike signs).

**Entry rule.** Pre-implementation data check REQUIRED: histogram the surprise metric to confirm headroom exists before full gauntlet. Metric: over trailing 48 bars, compute the mean mu and stddev sigma of |return|. Surprise of current bar = (|current return| - mu)/sigma. Fire when surprise > 3.0 AND the prior 24 bars had surprise-max < 1.5 (i.e. a quiet magnitude regime, then a genuinely novel large bar — the code-break). Direction = sign of the current bar's return. Enter in the direction of the breaking bar's close within its range (require close in top/bottom 25% consistent with sign). One fire per symbol per cooldown.

**Params.** `{"mag_window": 48, "surprise_thresh": 3.0, "prior_quiet_window": 24, "prior_quiet_max": 1.5, "close_position_pct": 0.25, "tp": 0.07, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 36}`

**Fire rate.** A 3-sigma magnitude bar preceded by 24 quiet bars: 3-sigma events on |return| occur ~0.5-1% of bars naively, but the 'preceded by quiet' condition (anti-correlated with vol clustering — a 3-sigma bar usually comes AFTER other large bars) makes the CONJUNCTION rare. Honest estimate from the clustering property: the quiet-then-surprise transition happens ~0.05-0.15/sym/day. Across 5 symbols ~0.3-0.75/day; over 930d n~280-700. The conjuncts (quiet prior + surprise now) are correlated NEGATIVELY, so I deliberately do NOT multiply them as independent — the estimate is the transition-rate directly, which the histogram will confirm.

**Fee survival.** A magnitude-regime birth is the START of a volatility cluster; realized moves in the following 36h during a fresh cluster empirically span multiples of the fee floor. The 36h horizon + 7% target exploits the horizon-lever: a volatility cluster resolves over hours-to-days, and the improved cost ratio (7% target vs 0.6% fee = ~12x) means even a modest hit rate clears fees. The entry-at-birth (not confirmed acceleration) avoids the r0 chasing penalty.

**Kill criterion.** Either (a) pre-check kills it: the surprise metric shows no headroom (p99 surprise < 3.0 impossible to reach, or quiet-then-surprise transition fires <0.01/sym/day = starved); or (b) full gauntlet expectancy < +0.4%/trade at n>=50. The sign of the breaking bar carrying no continuation edge (like post_shock_multiday_drift) is the decisive kill.

## 3. `hawkes_self_excitation_intensity_entry`  ·  lens: cross_domain_import

**Mechanism.** Source field: point-process statistics / seismology (Hawkes self-exciting processes, the formal generalization of Omori's law). Omori (r001) and epidemic-R0 (r002) both died because they entered ON the visible impulse — the midpoint. The Hawkes insight they missed: a self-exciting process has a measurable INTENSITY (lambda) that RISES BEFORE the next event, and the tradable moment is when intensity crosses its excitation threshold while the process is still in the EARLY branching phase (branching ratio just crossed 1 but cumulative event count is still low). Instead of entering on the big bar, enter when the intensity of large bars is rising but the cumulative displacement is still small — the process is loading, not spent. Not arbitraged: computing a rolling event-intensity with time-decay kernel is not something retail volatility tools do; they see ATR (a lagging average), not the branching-ratio phase of a self-exciting cluster.

**Lineage check.** Nearest deaths: omori_aftershock_ladder (aftershock persistence smaller than fees) and epidemic_r0_crossover_ignition ('marks the midpoint not the beginning'). This is NOT a descendant of either because both entered on/after the visible impulse (the mainshock, the R0 crossover). This enters EARLIER — during the intensity build-up when displacement is still small — explicitly targeting the phase before the move the prior two chased. The differentiator is the cumulative-displacement-low condition, which forces entry at the branching birth, not the spent cluster.

**Entry rule.** Define an event = bar with |return| > 1.5x the 96-bar median |return|. Compute Hawkes-style intensity: lambda = sum over last 12 bars of (event_indicator_i * exp(-0.3*(bars_ago_i))). Branching signal: lambda crosses above 1.5 (rising cluster) AND cumulative signed displacement over the excitation window (sum of returns over the 12 bars) is still SMALL in magnitude (< 1.0% absolute — the process is loading, not yet moved). Direction = sign of the majority of event bars in the window (net signed event flow). Enter that direction. One fire per symbol per cooldown.

**Params.** `{"event_mult": 1.5, "median_window": 96, "kernel_decay": 0.3, "intensity_window": 12, "intensity_thresh": 1.5, "cum_disp_max_pct": 1.0, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 24}`

**Fire rate.** Intensity crossing 1.5 with LOW cumulative displacement is the rare window: high intensity usually co-occurs with large displacement (they are positively correlated — this is the whole difficulty). So the conjunction 'intensity high AND displacement still small' is deliberately anti-correlated and rare — the loading phase is brief. Honest estimate: ~0.05-0.15/sym/day. Across 5 symbols ~0.3-0.75/day; 930d n~280-700. I explicitly do NOT multiply intensity-rate x low-displacement-rate as independent — they oppose; the estimate is the joint loading-window rate, which the data check should verify.

**Fee survival.** The thesis is that entering during the loading phase (before displacement) captures the FULL cluster move, not the retrace-harvested tail. If self-excitation is real, a cluster caught early delivers multiples of the fee floor; the whole point is that the prior imports lost because they entered late — this entry claims the missing amplitude by timing the branching birth. If the early-entry amplitude still doesn't clear 1%, the mechanism is refuted, not the fees.

**Kill criterion.** Expectancy < +0.4%/trade at n>=100 armed fires. Specifically, if the win rate is no better than entering ON the impulse (compare to the omori/r0 ~40% figures), then early intensity timing captures nothing the late entry didn't — the loading phase is not tradable and the Hawkes import joins Omori and R0 in the shock-family graveyard for good.

## 4. `one_sided_range_expansion_thrust`  ·  lens: microstructure_from_ohlcv

**Mechanism.** Absorption died because 'high volume + small body' is anti-correlated in crypto (r002 empty set). The correct OHLCV microstructure signal for a one-sided book is the OPPOSITE geometry: high volume + LARGE body + wick asymmetry all pointing the same way. When one side of the book is exhausted, price expands its range with the CLOSE pinned at the extreme (tiny wick in the direction of travel, no rejection) on elevated volume — the book cleared with no opposing flow. This is the footprint of a genuine imbalance, and it is a CONTINUATION shape (not a fade), so it aligns with the +5/-3 asymmetric exit. Not arbitraged: retail sees a 'big green candle' and either chases late or fades it; the specific conjunction of large body + minimal same-direction wick + volume + the close being an all-time-recent extreme is a selective slice that most screens don't isolate, and its selectivity keeps fire rate low enough to survive the slot constraint.

**Lineage check.** Nearest deaths: absorption_shelf_breakout (r002, absorption invisible — high vol + small body is the empty set) and failed_auction_rejection_wick (r001, fade-shaped, wicks don't identify trapped flow). This is the DELIBERATE INVERSION of both: it demands high vol + LARGE body (which are POSITIVELY correlated, so the conjunction is populated, not empty), and it is continuation-shaped (close at extreme, small wick), not a fade of a wick rejection. It engineers around the exact correlation structure that killed absorption.

**Entry rule.** Fire long when: current bar body (|close-open|) > 2.0x the median body of prior 96 bars AND volume > 1.5x median volume of prior 96 bars AND the upper wick (high-close) < 0.15x the body (close pinned near high, one-sided) AND lower wick (open-low) < 0.4x body (little opposing probe) AND close > max(high) of prior 12 bars (fresh local extreme). Symmetric short for down-bars. One fire per symbol per cooldown. Entry at next bar open (continuation, not limit).

**Params.** `{"body_mult": 2.0, "vol_mult": 1.5, "median_window": 96, "same_dir_wick_max": 0.15, "opp_wick_max": 0.4, "extreme_lookback": 12, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 24}`

**Fire rate.** Large-body (>2x median) high-vol (>1.5x median) bars are positively correlated so the pair is populated: ~2-4% of bars have >2x body. The wick-geometry filters (tiny same-dir wick, small opp wick, fresh extreme) cut this to ~15-25% of large-body bars. Net ~0.5-1.5/sym/day. Across 5 symbols ~2.5-7/day; 930d n~2000-5000 raw. This is a HIGH fire rate — the cooldown and 5-slot ceiling will bind, so effective fires ~1-2/day booked. Ample sample; the risk is over-firing, not starvation.

**Fee survival.** A one-sided range-expansion thrust is a follow-through signal by construction — the +5/-3 exit rewards exactly this shape (r001 lesson: exits reward follow-through). The claim is that a cleared-book thrust continues far enough to hit +6% within 24h more than the fade shapes did. If the amplitude of continuation after a clean one-sided thrust doesn't clear the fee floor, then OHLCV microstructure carries no exploitable imbalance signal and the lens is done.

**Kill criterion.** Expectancy < +0.4%/trade at n>=200 (it fires plenty, so n is not the issue). If a clean one-sided thrust continues no better than a random large bar (win rate near the exit's breakeven ~38%), the geometry is decorative and the microstructure-from-OHLCV lens is refuted for continuation as it was for absorption/fades.

## 5. `round_number_breach_continuation`  ·  lens: behavioral_calendar

**Mechanism.** Round-number OVERSHOOT-SNAP (fade) died in r001. But the round-number LEVEL still carries behavioral structure — the failure was the fade direction, not the magnet. Stop clusters and resting limit orders concentrate at round numbers (whole thousands for BTC, whole hundreds for ETH/SOL, whole dollars/tenths for LINK/AVAX). When price BREACHES a round number decisively (closes through it with momentum after coiling near it), the stop cascade on the far side ADDS energy to the move — a continuation, not a reversion. This is calendar-behavioral but PRICE-level, not clock-time, so it sidesteps the 4-for-4 named-time-coil death entirely (the epitaph is specifically about clock times lending coils no energy; a price level with resting stops is a different mechanism). Not arbitraged: retail knows round numbers matter but trades the bounce (the dead fade); the continuation-through-the-breach with stop-cascade fuel is the harder, less-crowded side.

**Lineage check.** Nearest deaths: round_number_overshoot_snap (r001, fade-shaped, under-fired 5-8x) and the 4 named-time coil deaths (dead_zone, options_expiry, weekend, asian_london). This is NOT the overshoot-snap because it is CONTINUATION through the level (stop-cascade fuel) not a fade of the overshoot — the exact inversion of the dead direction. It is NOT a named-time coil because the trigger is a PRICE level (round number with resting orders), not a clock time — the epitaph explicitly blames the clock for lending no energy; a stop cluster at a price is a real order-flow reservoir, not a calendar artifact.

**Entry rule.** Identify nearest round number: BTC per $1000, ETH per $100, SOL per $10, LINK per $1, AVAX per $1 (scale-appropriate). Fire long when: price spent >= 6 of the last 12 bars within 0.3% of a round level R (coiling/magnetism) AND current bar closes > R by more than 0.4% (decisive breach) AND current bar volume > 1.3x median of prior 96 bars (cascade participation) AND close in top 25% of bar range. Symmetric short on downside breach. Entry next bar open. One fire per symbol per cooldown.

**Params.** `{"proximity_pct": 0.3, "coil_bars_required": 6, "coil_window": 12, "breach_pct": 0.4, "vol_mult": 1.3, "close_position_pct": 0.25, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 24}`

**Fire rate.** How often does price coil near a round level then breach it? Round levels are hit routinely (price crosses a $1000 BTC level or $10 SOL level several times/week in trending phases). The coil-then-breach conjunction: coiling near a level happens ~0.2-0.4/sym/day, and a decisive breach given coiling ~30-40% of those (many coils fail/re-coil). Net ~0.06-0.15/sym/day. Across 5 symbols ~0.3-0.75/day; 930d n~280-700. The coil and breach are sequentially dependent (breach is conditional on prior coil), NOT independent — I estimate the joint sequence rate directly, avoiding the conjunction-multiplication error.

**Fee survival.** The mechanism IS the amplitude claim: a stop cascade on the far side of a breached level provides forced-flow fuel, driving the continuation past the fee floor — the resting-order reservoir is what makes this move larger than an ordinary coil break (which died under fees as dead_zone). If breaches with cascade participation (elevated volume) don't move >1% more than plain coil breaks, the stop-cascade fuel is imaginary and this is just another coil death.

**Kill criterion.** Expectancy < +0.4%/trade at n>=100. Decisively: if round-number breaches with volume confirmation perform no better than the dead dead_zone/asian_london coil breaks (which resolved at random vs the fee floor), then round numbers carry no continuation energy either and the behavioral-calendar price-level thesis is refuted alongside its clock-time cousins.
