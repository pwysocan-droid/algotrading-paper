## 1. `liquidity_window_shock_fade_maker`  ·  lens: cost_engineering

**Mechanism.** The one measured multi-day edge in this archive is CONTRARIAN: sign-aligned forward returns after 3%/3x-vol shock bars are -3 to -4% at 24-120h (n=53, control arm). The cost-engineering job is to harvest that fade WITHOUT paying the volatility-conditional slippage tax that killed impulse-chasers — slippage is worst exactly at the shock, so a chasing taker entry on the shock bar is the worst possible fill. Instead, place a resting maker order on the SAME SIDE as the eventual fade (i.e. counter to the shock) that fills only if price extends the shock a further fraction in the next few bars. Because the population is 'price overshoots then reverts,' the extension-to-fill is NOT adverse selection (unlike the r003 retest, where the return-to-level was the failure signal): here the further overshoot is exactly the mispricing deepening, and the maker fill lands at a better price on the trades that revert hardest. Price comes to you at the extreme; the fill happens during the volatile window at a limit price you set, not at a chased market price.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death is post_shock_multiday_drift (r003) and pullback_to_breakout_level_limit (r003). This is NOT a descendant of either: post_shock traded shock direction (continuation) and was overturned — the real signal is contrarian, which this trades. It is NOT the r003 retest limit because that waited at a broken level where return-to-level selected failures (adverse selection confirmed both arms); here the maker fill is triggered by FURTHER extension of an overshoot in a population measured to mean-revert, so waiting selects the deepest mispricings, not the failures. It fades, but the fade is admissible per the 2026-07-19 control-arm measurement and the exit is matched to the thesis (contrarian tp beyond the fade, wide time stop).

**Entry rule.** Detect a shock bar: |close-open|/open >= shock_pct AND bar true-range >= vol_mult * median(TR, 96 bars). On the shock bar's direction (say up), place a resting BUY-side-of-fade order = SHORT-direction maker: submit a limit to enter SHORT at price = shock_close * (1 + extend_frac) valid for fill_window bars; symmetric for a down shock (limit LONG at shock_close*(1-extend_frac)). Enter only if filled within fill_window bars; else cancel. Exit: contrarian tp toward the reversion, wide protective stop, multi-day time exit.

**Params.** `{"shock_pct": 0.03, "vol_mult": 3.0, "median_lookback_bars": 96, "extend_frac": 0.006, "fill_window": 6, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.04, "time_exit_hours": 96}`

**Fire rate.** Base rate of shock bars from the control-arm measurement: ~53 shock bars in 930d population BEFORE the extension filter, i.e. ~0.011/sym/day raw. That alone gives only n~53 — inadmissible if every shock required extension. So loosen shock_pct to 0.02 and vol_mult to 2.5: the r004 return_magnitude and one_sided_range work measured comparable large-bar events at 0.68-0.73/sym/day. Take a conservative 0.25/sym/day for the shock trigger = 0.25*5*930 = ~1160 triggers. The extension-to-fill maker condition (a further 0.6% move within 6 bars) fills on roughly 15-25% of triggers given 5-min crypto range — call it 0.18: 1160*0.18 = ~209 placed trades. Decidable at n~200.

**Fee survival.** The measured fade is -3 to -4% sign-aligned over 24-120h; entering AFTER a further 0.6% extension improves the entry another ~0.6% into the mispricing, so the expected favorable move is ~3.5-4.5% at 96h — 6-7x the 0.6% round-trip. Maker entry halves the fee on the fill side. The horizon lever (96h vs 24h) plus the largest gross effect ever measured here gives ~10x cost ratio, the admissible territory per the measured ceiling.

**Kill criterion.** Net expectancy < +0.5%/trade at n>=100, OR the fill-side maker order proves to be adverse selection (extension-filled trades show WORSE reversion than a matched taker-on-shock-bar control), replicating the r003 retest failure. Either kills it decisively.

## 2. `cross_sectional_dispersion_gate_trend_engine`  ·  lens: gate_engine_pairing

**Mechanism.** The self-referential-outcome gate family is CLOSED — no gate may condition on its own past trade outcomes without a named new information source. When basket returns are highly correlated and dispersion is LOW, a directional move in BTC is a market-wide factor move that the alts must follow — a with-trend continuation entry on any symbol is amplified because the driving force is systemic, not idiosyncratic noise that mean-reverts. The gate ('is the basket currently in a synchronized-directional regime?') carries information no single symbol's own OHLCV stream contains, and it is a SINGLE scalar condition (dispersion below a threshold), avoiding the conjunctive-rarity that starved every prior gate. This is the CANARY continuation-shaped spec for the round: it bets on continuation, so a negative result is the regime-flip alarm.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death is placebo_streak_gated_weekly_trend_engine (r005) and constraint_rejection_pressure_release_engine (r006). NOT a descendant: both prior gates conditioned on the system's OWN trade outcomes (placebo hit-rate, self-referential system_state) — the closed family. This gate conditions on cross-sectional dispersion of the basket, an external measurement absent from any engine's trade stream, satisfying the 2026-07-19 reopening bar (a named mechanism carrying info the ungated stream lacks). It differs from r006's synchronization-count gate (which over-fired at 69% co-occurrence and was a CURRENT-bar breakout nested in a sync window) by gating a slower with-trend engine on a continuous scalar and NOT requiring same-bar breakout co-occurrence.

**Entry rule.** Declare btc_bars context. Each bar compute cross-sectional dispersion = stdev across the 5 symbols of their trailing-N-bar returns. **Params.** `{"disp_lookback_bars": 48, "disp_pctile": 30, "disp_roll_days": 20, "trend_lookback": 96, "return_sign_bars": 288, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 48}`

Gate open 30% of the time and INDEPENDENT of any single breakout (dispersion is a basket property, breakout is a symbol property — the anti-correlation risk is low because low dispersion does not preclude a single symbol breaking out; if anything they co-occur in factor moves, raising the rate): 1.0 * 0.30 * 5 * 930 = ~1395 armed candidates. ## 3. `kolmogorov_directional_asymmetry_break`  ·  lens: information_theory

**Mechanism.** Signs are maxed out at H~0.95/1.0 (dead both directions r001/r002) and magnitude-surprise identifies volatility birth but not direction (r004). The untested information-theoretic quantity is DIRECTIONAL COMPRESSIBILITY ASYMMETRY: encode the recent up-move magnitudes and down-move magnitudes as two separate sequences and compare their compressibility (approximate via the ratio of their sample entropies / Lempel-Ziv complexity). When up-magnitudes have become MORE compressible (more regular, repeating similar-sized up-steps = orderly accumulation) while down-magnitudes remain incompressible (random, sparse = no organized selling), the book is one-sided in a way sign-entropy cannot see, because sign-entropy discards magnitude and magnitude-surprise discards the up/down split. This is a joint magnitude-AND-direction structure, the one corner of the information lens not yet closed. Direction of entry = the side that is MORE compressible (more organized).

**Lineage check.** Nearest death is return_magnitude_compressibility_break (r004) and conditional_entropy_regime_expansion (r002). NOT a descendant: r004 measured compressibility of the UNSIGNED magnitude stream and asked it for a sign (it couldn't give one — headroom existed but no direction). This measures the DIFFERENCE in compressibility BETWEEN the up and down magnitude sub-streams and takes direction from WHICH side is more organized — a signed asymmetry, not an unsigned surprise. It differs from r002 (sign entropy, mathematically saturated) by operating on magnitudes split by sign, never on the sign sequence itself.

**Entry rule.** Over lookback bars, split bar returns into up-magnitudes and down-magnitudes. Compute LZ-complexity (or normalized sample entropy) of each quantized sub-stream: C_up, C_down. Define asymmetry A = (C_down - C_up)/(C_down + C_up). Enter LONG when A > asym_thresh (up-stream markedly MORE compressible = organized accumulation) AND current close > close[lookback] (with-trend confirmation, so this is a continuation not a fade). Symmetric SHORT when A < -asym_thresh and downtrend. Fixed exits.

**Params.** `{"lookback": 192, "quantize_bins": 8, "asym_thresh": 0.2, "tp": 0.06, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 48}`

**Fire rate.** PREMISE CHECK MANDATED before gauntlet: histogram A over all bars/symbols; the |A|>0.20 tail must be non-empty and not anti-correlated with the trend-confirmation condition. Arithmetic: A is a continuous statistic; |A|>0.20 is roughly the outer ~25-30% of a symmetric-ish distribution (organized regimes are common, per the r001 lesson that low-entropy states are the NORM). With-trend confirmation holds ~50% of the time and is only mildly anti-correlated with asymmetry (an organized up-stream tends to co-occur with an uptrend — POSITIVELY correlated, raising joint rate). Conservative joint 0.15/bar-eligibility but apply 1h cooldown: cap at ~0.4/sym/day placed = 0.4*5*930 = ~1860, haircut 5x for calibration = ~372. Decidable. If the premise histogram shows the |A|>0.20 tail empty, the idea is killed pre-gauntlet (entropy-ceiling lesson).

**Fee survival.** If organized-accumulation asymmetry precedes directional continuation, the 48h/+6% target gives 10x cost ratio. The mechanism claims to read book one-sidedness from magnitude organization — if real, moves should exceed 1%.

**Kill criterion.** Premise-check: |A|>asym_thresh tail is empty or fires <100 (mathematically absent, like r002). Post-gauntlet: win rate < 40% breakeven for +6/-3 at n>=100 (asymmetry organizes but does not predict direction, replicating r004).

## 4. `epidemic_susceptible_depletion_terminal_burst`  ·  lens: cross_domain_import

**Mechanism.** SOURCE FIELD: epidemiology, SIR final-size / susceptible-depletion dynamics. Prior imports (Omori decay r001, R0 growth r002, Hawkes loading r004) all tried to enter DURING self-excitation and failed because at 5-min scale that phase is either spent or the market's default state. SIR predicts something none of them modeled: an epidemic ENDS not with a whimper but when the susceptible pool DEPLETES — the final burst of infections happens as the last susceptibles are consumed, and the curve then collapses because there is no one left to infect. Mapped to price: a sustained directional move consumes 'susceptible' resting liquidity/late entrants; the observable signature of depletion is that each successive push produces DECREASING volume per unit of price displacement (fewer susceptibles converting per contact). When volume-per-displacement has been monotonically falling across a multi-bar run WHILE price still extends, the pool is depleting — the move is terminal and the CONTRARIAN reversion is imminent. This is a FADE, admissible because it aligns with the measured multi-day reversion signature, and the entry is at the exhaustion point, not chasing the impulse.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death is omori_aftershock_ladder (r001), epidemic_r0_crossover_ignition (r002), hawkes_self_excitation_intensity_entry (r004) — the point-process import family, 3-for-3 dead. NOT a descendant: all three entered to CONTINUE a cluster/ignition (continuation bets that chased). This models the epidemic's TERMINATION via susceptible depletion and enters CONTRARIAN at exhaustion — the opposite lifecycle phase and opposite direction. It also differs from predator_prey_volume_depletion_rebound (r006) which used raw volume-decline-over-up-days (measured at baseline 0.162, no edge); this uses volume-per-DISPLACEMENT (efficiency), a ratio, not raw volume count, and enters on falling conversion-efficiency, a distinct signature.

**Entry rule.** Over a run of run_bars consecutive same-direction bars (net |return| >= run_pct), compute per-bar efficiency e_i = |return_i| / volume_i. Require efficiency RISING (each push moves price more per unit volume = fewer susceptibles left to absorb it): e monotonically increasing over the last decline_bars of the run AND cumulative run volume falling. This is depletion. Enter CONTRARIAN (against the run) at run's end. Fixed exits matched to reversion.

**Params.** `{"run_bars": 8, "run_pct": 0.02, "decline_bars": 4, "tp": 0.05, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 48}`

**Fire rate.** PREMISE CHECK MANDATED: histogram P(efficiency monotonically rising over 4 bars | 8-bar directional run >=2%) — must not be the empty set and must not be anti-correlated. Monotone-rising efficiency over 4 bars is ~ (1/24) if random but efficiency is autocorrelated in trending exhaustion so realistically ~0.15-0.25 conditional. Take run rate 0.6/sym/day * 0.18 = 0.108/sym/day placed = 0.108*5*930 = ~502. Haircut 3x for calibration = ~167. Decidable. If premise histogram shows the conjunction empty (efficiency-rising anti-correlated with directional runs), killed pre-gauntlet.

## 5. `trapped_breakout_volume_void_reversal`  ·  lens: microstructure_from_ohlcv

**Mechanism.** The OHLCV candle-geometry lens is 5-for-5 dead (wick, absorption, gap, one-sided thrust, compression-gap) — every shape reading a SINGLE bar's or two bars' geometry. The untried structure is a MULTI-BAR volume-void reversal that reads the ORDER of volume relative to price extension, aligned with the measured reversion signature. Mechanism: a breakout to a fresh N-bar extreme that occurs on LOW volume relative to the bars that built the level is a breakout into a VOID — no resting liquidity was consumed because none was there, meaning the move is not backed by real flow and the participants who chased the breakout are trapped above/below a shelf with no volume support. When price then closes back inside the prior range (re-entry), the trapped chasers must exit into the void, driving a sharp contrarian move. Unlike a fade of a wick (r001) or a snap of an overshoot, the entry trigger is the RE-ENTRY CLOSE back into range combined with the diagnostic that the breakout volume was anomalously LOW — a specific trapped-flow signature that candle geometry alone (body/wick) cannot express, requiring the volume-vs-level history.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death is one_sided_range_expansion_thrust (r004) and failed_auction_rejection_wick (r001), plus range_compression_then_directional_expansion_gap (r006). NOT a descendant: r004 and r001 keyed on a single bar's body/wick at an extreme (geometry); this keys on the RELATIONSHIP between breakout volume and the volume that built the level (a low-volume breakout is the void diagnostic), plus a re-entry close — a two-phase flow-order signature, not one bar's shape. It fades, but per FADE-SHAPED it must justify surviving asymmetric exits: it does, because the trap-into-void reversal aligns with the measured contrarian multi-day signature and the entry is on confirmed re-entry (the trapped exits are forced flow, continuation of the reversal, not a bounce fade).

**Entry rule.** Detect breakout: close > highest-high(level_lookback bars) [or < lowest-low for short setup]. Diagnose void: breakout-bar volume < void_frac * median(volume, level_lookback bars) — the break consumed abnormally LITTLE volume. Then WAIT up to trap_window bars for RE-ENTRY: a bar closes back below the broken high (above broken low). Enter CONTRARIAN on the re-entry close (short after failed up-break into void; long after failed down-break). Fixed exits.

**Params.** `{"level_lookback": 96, "void_frac": 0.6, "trap_window": 6, "tp": 0.05, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 36}`
