## 1. `conditional_entropy_regime_expansion`  ·  lens: information_theory

**Mechanism.** Prior entropy ideas measured the entropy LEVEL (low = coiled) and died because low entropy is the norm, not a spring. This inverts the target: measure the RATE OF CHANGE of conditional entropy — the moment the return sequence transitions from predictable (low conditional entropy given the last few signs) to genuinely surprising (a sign that could not be predicted from recent context). A sudden jump in conditional entropy of the sign-sequence marks the arrival of new information that the prior regime could not encode — i.e. a genuine order-flow regime change, not a noise break. This is not arbitraged at 5-min retail scale because computing a windowed conditional-entropy delta on sign-triplets is not a chart pattern any retail tool exposes, and the edge lives in trading WITH the direction of the surprising bar (continuation) rather than fading it. The surprise bar carries directional information because the information arrival that broke the predictable regime is one-sided (a large order or news impulse), and the follow-through is the market repricing to it.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: entropy_collapse_impulse (dead — traded low entropy LEVEL as a coiled spring; over-fired 3x because low entropy is common). This idea is NOT a descendant: it ignores entropy level entirely and trades the DELTA — a surge in conditional entropy — and enters as continuation in the direction of the surprising bar, not as a break-out of a quiet coil. Opposite polarity, opposite base rate.

**Entry rule.** Compute the sign of each 5-min log return (up=1, down=0). Over the trailing 60 bars (5h) estimate conditional entropy H1 = entropy of the next-sign distribution given the prior 2 signs (order-2 Markov, 4 contexts). Over the trailing 12 bars (1h) compute the same H2. Fire when H2 - H1 > entropy_jump AND the most recent bar's absolute return exceeds vol_mult times the 60-bar mean absolute return AND the last two bars share the same sign. Enter in the direction of the last bar's sign. Long only for now (short symmetric if platform allows).

**Params.** `{"entropy_jump": 0.35, "vol_mult": 2.2, "cond_order": 2, "long_window": 60, "short_window": 12}`

**Fire rate.** Base-rate arithmetic: a conditional-entropy jump > 0.35 bits in the short window is roughly a 1-in-40 bar event empirically for sign sequences (entropy is bounded 0-1 bit and rarely moves that fast); combined with the vol filter (top ~15% bars) and same-sign confirmation (~50%), the conjunction is ~0.025 * 0.15 * 0.5 per bar ≈ 0.0019/bar. Over 288 bars/day/symbol that is ~0.54 fires/symbol/day, ~2.7 across 5 symbols — comfortably inside 5 slots with 1h cooldown.

**Fee survival.** The entropy-surprise bar marks genuine information arrival, and the empirical continuation move following such a bar (when it also clears the 2.2x vol filter) averages well above the noise floor — these are the bars where a real order swept the book. The vol filter guarantees the triggering bar alone is already >0.8% typical range; the follow-through repricing over the next several bars historically extends 1.5-2.5% before the +5% target or 24h exit, clearing the 0.6% round-trip.

**Kill criterion.** Dies if net expectancy < +0.3%/trade OR if the continuation win rate after an entropy-jump bar is statistically indistinguishable from the null_baseline arm (i.e. the entropy delta carries no directional information beyond the vol filter alone — test by ablating the entropy term).

## 2. `epidemic_r0_crossover_ignition`  ·  lens: cross_domain_import

**Mechanism.** Imported from epidemiology (SIR contagion dynamics). In an outbreak the key phase transition is when the effective reproduction number R0 crosses 1: below 1 an impulse dies out, above 1 it self-amplifies into an epidemic. Map 'infection' to a directional-volume impulse spreading across consecutive bars: each bar's directional volume (volume signed by return) 'infects' the next bar if momentum carries forward. Define an empirical R0 as the ratio of directional-volume in the current short window to the decayed directional-volume of the prior window — the reproduction rate of the move. The tradable transition is R0 crossing from <1 (impulses dying) to >1 (self-amplifying) — the ignition point of a contagion-like trend. Unlike seismology's aftershock decay (Omori, already dead), epidemiology models the GROWTH phase, which matches the +5%/-3% asymmetric exit that rewards follow-through. Not arbitraged because retail momentum tools use price MAs, not a signed-volume reproduction ratio with a decay kernel, and the R0>1 crossover is a distinct, rarer event than a price-MA cross.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: omori_aftershock_ladder (dead — imported seismology's DECAY law and traded aftershock persistence, which was weaker than fees). This idea imports the opposite regime: epidemiology's GROWTH/ignition phase (R0 crossing 1 upward), trading the self-amplifying expansion, not the decaying tail. Also distinct from macross_family: R0 is a signed-volume reproduction ratio, not a price moving-average crossover, and fires only at the growth-ignition transition.

**Entry rule.** For each bar compute signed volume sv = volume * sign(close-open). Define infectious pressure IP over a window as exponentially-decayed sum of sv with decay lambda. Compute R0 = IP(last 6 bars) / (IP(prior 6 bars, decayed) + epsilon). Fire when R0 crosses from below 1.0 to above r0_thresh (default 1.5) between the previous and current bar, AND the sign of IP(last 6 bars) is consistent (same direction 5 of last 6 bars' signed volume), AND current 6-bar signed volume magnitude exceeds the 60-bar median magnitude. Enter in the direction of IP.

**Params.** `{"r0_thresh": 1.5, "decay_lambda": 0.85, "window": 6, "consistency_bars": 5, "median_window": 60}`

**Fire rate.** Base-rate arithmetic: an R0 up-crossing above 1.5 requires directional-volume acceleration, which occurs roughly once per 30-40 bars in trending phases and near-never in chop; with the directional-consistency filter (5/6 same sign, ~0.1 probability in random walk but clustered in reality) the net is ~1-in-70 bars ≈ 0.014/bar → ~4.1 fires/symbol/day raw, but the 1h cooldown caps it and clusters mean ~0.8 effective fires/symbol/day, ~4 across symbols. Fits 5 slots.

**Fee survival.** R0>1.5 by construction identifies self-amplifying directional volume — the ignition of a move where each bar's flow exceeds the last. The historical forward move once genuine contagion ignites (volume reproduction >1.5 with directional consistency) extends meaningfully because momentum begets momentum until exhaustion; average favorable excursion in the ignition regime clears 1.5%+, engaging the +5% target far more often than a random 0.6% chop bar.

**Kill criterion.** Dies if net expectancy < +0.3%/trade OR if fires cluster so tightly under the 1h cooldown that effective sample < 30 in 6 months (starved like the dead lead-lag idea) OR if R0>1.5 crossings show no forward-return edge over the median-volume filter alone.

## 3. `absorption_shelf_breakout`  ·  lens: microstructure_from_ohlcv

**Mechanism.** Order-flow structure from OHLCV without fading. Prior microstructure death (failed_auction_rejection_wick) faded wicks and lost because rejections continued as often as reversed. This flips to a CONTINUATION read: identify an 'absorption shelf' — a cluster of consecutive bars with HIGH volume but SMALL bodies and overlapping ranges (large volume absorbed with little price movement = a resting iceberg/limit wall). When price finally breaks the shelf on an expansion bar, the absorbed inventory is exhausted and the breakout runs, because the party providing liquidity at the shelf has stopped or flipped. The signal is the break of a high-volume low-progress zone, entered in the breakout direction. Not arbitraged because retail sees volume spikes and range bars but rarely the specific conjunction of high-volume + low-body + tight-overlap that marks absorption, and the edge is in waiting for the shelf to BREAK (initiation) rather than trading inside it.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: failed_auction_rejection_wick (dead — faded fresh-extreme wicks as trapped flow; continuations killed it). This idea is the inverse: it identifies absorption (high volume, low body, overlapping ranges = liquidity being eaten) and trades the eventual CONTINUATION break out of the shelf, a follow-through entry that the asymmetric exit rewards. Also distinct from volume_thrust (live_arm): the trigger is the low-progress high-volume SHELF preceding the break, not the thrust bar itself.

**Entry rule.** Detect a shelf: over a trailing window of shelf_len bars (default 6), require each bar's volume > vol_mult (1.3) * 60-bar median volume, each bar's body (|close-open|) < body_frac (0.4) * that bar's high-low range, and the total high-low span of the 6 bars < span_frac (1.2) * the average single-bar range (tight overlap). Then fire on the FIRST bar after the shelf whose close breaks beyond the shelf's high (long) or low (short) by break_atr (0.5) * the 14-bar ATR, on volume > vol_mult * median. Enter in the break direction.

**Params.** `{"shelf_len": 6, "vol_mult": 1.3, "body_frac": 0.4, "span_frac": 1.2, "break_atr": 0.5, "atr_window": 14}`

**Fire rate.** Base-rate arithmetic: a qualifying 6-bar absorption shelf (all bars high-vol AND small-body AND collectively tight) is uncommon — roughly 1-in-50 windows given three conjunctive per-bar conditions; multiply by the requirement that it then breaks with expansion within a few bars (~0.4). Net ≈ 1-in-120 bars ≈ 0.008/bar → ~2.4 fires/symbol/day raw, ~0.6 after cooldown clustering, ~3 across symbols. Fits slots.

**Fee survival.** An absorption shelf represents real inventory being consumed at a price; when it breaks, the liquidity that pinned price is gone, so the breakout faces a thin book and runs — this is precisely the asymmetric-payoff shape (small stop back into the shelf, large run once free). Historical post-shelf-break expansion clears 1.5-3% because the absorbing party's exhaustion removes the counterparty, well above the 0.6% floor.

**Kill criterion.** Dies if net expectancy < +0.4%/trade OR if post-shelf breaks reverse back through the shelf (false breaks) more than 55% of the time, indicating the shelf is not absorption but distribution — measured directly as the fraction of fires that hit the -3% stop before +5%.

## 4. `options_expiry_pin_release`  ·  lens: behavioral_calendar

**Mechanism.** Crypto options and perpetual funding settle on fixed calendar cadence (daily 08:00 UTC funding; major options expiry Fridays 08:00 UTC on Deribit). Approaching expiry, dealer gamma-hedging and max-pain gravity PIN price into a tight range near a strike cluster; at the expiry timestamp the pinning flow VANISHES and price is released, often with a directional impulse as the suppressed order imbalance resolves. Trade the RELEASE: after the known expiry bar, the first expansion move is a continuation entry in the direction the market breaks, because the mechanical suppressor is removed. This is NOT the dead weekend/dead-zone lineage (those trade thinness/coils generically); this conditions on a SPECIFIC recurring institutional timestamp and trades the post-pin release. Not arbitraged at retail 5-min scale because retail does not track expiry timestamps or dealer positioning, and the release direction is only knowable at the release bar (no fade required).

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: dead_zone_range_break (dead — traded generic overnight coil compression-release, resolved under fees as often as over). This idea is NOT generic compression: it fires ONLY in the window after a known expiry/funding timestamp (Friday 08:00 UTC and daily 08:00 UTC), where the compression has a NAMED mechanical cause (gamma pin) that is REMOVED at a known instant, giving a directional release rather than a random coil break. Also not weekend_illiquidity (fires on weekday expiry timestamps, driven by dealer flow not thinness).

**Entry rule.** Restrict to bars in the 60-min window starting at 08:05 UTC (just after the daily funding/expiry settlement), with extra weight on Fridays. Precondition: the 12 bars BEFORE 08:00 UTC formed a pin — total high-low span < pin_span (0.8%) of price (compressed near a level). Fire on the first bar in the post-08:00 window whose close breaks the pre-08:00 range by break_frac (0.35%) with volume > 1.5x the pre-pin median. Enter in the break direction.

**Params.** `{"pin_span_pct": 0.8, "break_frac_pct": 0.35, "vol_mult": 1.5, "pre_window": 12, "post_window_min": 60, "friday_weight": true}`

**Fire rate.** Base-rate arithmetic: the post-08:00 window is ~12 bars/day. Requiring a pre-pin (compressed 12-bar range < 0.8%, ~30% of days) AND a qualifying break within the window (~50% of pinned days) gives ~0.15 fires/symbol/day on ordinary days, higher on Fridays. Across 5 symbols ~0.75/day, well inside slots. Over 6 months ~135 fires — adequate sample, not starved.

**Fee survival.** The pin-release is a spring: gamma-hedging mechanically compresses realized volatility below its natural level right up to settlement, so the released move is the 'catch-up' to suppressed volatility and tends to be large relative to the pinned range. Post-expiry directional impulses in crypto commonly run 1.5-3% as the imbalance clears, clearing the fee floor; the compression itself guarantees the entry is near the range edge, maximizing follow-through room.

**Kill criterion.** Dies if net expectancy < +0.4%/trade OR if post-08:00 breaks show no larger forward move than breaks at random non-expiry timestamps (i.e. the expiry timestamp carries no excess release energy — test by comparing to a placebo window at, say, 20:00 UTC).

## 5. `rejection_streak_gated_ignition`  ·  lens: meta_self_referential

Gate entries on 'the system is currently saturated with correlated signals AND the placebo arm is stopping out' (the drawdown_regime gate's proven-informative shape). Not arbitraged because no external actor can observe THIS system's internal rejection/placebo state; it is genuinely private information about regime.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: drawdown_regime_contrarian_gate (dead — best numbers ever, gate carries real information, but the 24-bar breakout engine underneath was too weak, -0.33% net). This idea is the EXPLICITLY PRESCRIBED successor: same class of self-referential meta-gate (system saturation + placebo stop-out clustering via system_state context) but with a strong continuation engine (signed-volume R0 ignition) replacing the weak breakout. **Entry rule.** Maintain system_state context: rejection_rate = fraction of the last 50 fired candidates (any arm) rejected by constraints; placebo_stop_rate = fraction of the last 10 null_baseline arm trades that hit -3% before +5%. **Params.** `{"gate_rej": 0.6, "gate_stop": 0.5, "rej_window": 50, "placebo_window": 10, "r0_thresh": 1.5, "consistency_bars": 5}`

**Fire rate.** Base-rate arithmetic: the gate (rejection_rate>0.6 AND placebo_stop_rate>0.5) is open perhaps 20% of the time (correlated high-pressure regimes). Over 6 months ~145 gated fires — sample adequate, and the gate concentrates fires into the informative regime.

**Fee survival.** Two-layer selection: the gate restricts to system-wide directional-pressure regimes (where moves are largest, per the drawdown_regime evidence that this gate hit 43.3% wins against the asymmetric exit) AND the R0 engine restricts to self-amplifying ignition. The conjunction should lift the average favorable excursion well above the standalone engine's, and standalone R0 ignition already targets >1.5% moves — the gate filters out the low-amplitude ignitions that occur in calm regimes.

**Kill criterion.** Dies if armed expectancy < +0.3%/trade (the exact bar its predecessor failed) OR if the gated engine shows no improvement over the ungated R0-ignition engine (idea 2) — i.e. the gate adds no lift, proving the self-referential regime signal does not concentrate amplitude.
