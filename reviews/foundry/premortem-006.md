# Pre-mortem: Round 006

Blind review of `reviews/foundry/round-006.json` against `reviews/foundry/dead-ideas.json` (33 prior deaths, 22 failure lessons). Goal: kill on paper what can be killed on paper, before any gauntlet spend. Calibration prior applied throughout (2026-07-19 regression, 18 gradient records): **predicted fire rates carry ~zero information** — specs predicting ≥0.3 fires/sym/day over-predicted ~17x on average, specs predicting <0.3/day under-predicted ~2x on average, with ±8x scatter either way. A spec's own arithmetic being internally tidy does not make its prediction trustworthy; only the shape of the conjunction (independent vs. correlated, single-condition vs. multi-condition-across-a-window) says anything about which direction the miss will run.

## 1. `predator_prey_volume_depletion_rebound` — **REDESIGN**

**One change that fixes it:** re-derive the volume-monotonic-decline conditional probability directly against the data (a histogram of `sign(vol_day[-1]-vol_day[-2]), sign(vol_day[-2]-vol_day[-3])` conditioned on 3-up-days) before spending a gauntlet slot — the spec's own stated number is internally inconsistent with the direction of its own argument.

- **Arithmetic error, not just calibration risk:** the spec asserts "volume-decline-into-rising-price is a NEGATIVELY correlated pairing... so conditional prob is lower, ~0.25-0.35." But the unconditioned combinatorial base rate for 3 arbitrary volume values falling in strictly monotonic-decreasing order is **1/3! = 1/6 ≈ 0.167** — one ordering out of six. If the pairing is genuinely negatively correlated with up-days (as the spec itself claims, and as is plausible since up-trends often bring participation/volume), the conditional probability should be *pulled below* 0.167, not landed at 0.25-0.35, which is **1.5-2x above** the naive random baseline. The spec says "lower" and then states a number that is higher than the relevant reference point it never names. This is exactly the "5-minute histogram would have killed it" opportunity the CONJUNCTIONS MULTIPLY TO ZERO lesson calls for — the fix is cheap (a direct frequency count on daily-aggregated bars) and should happen before implementation, not after a 930-day gauntlet burns a slot on a miscalibrated premise.
- **Decidability is a real concern if the true rate is lower:** the ~116-fire estimate already sits close to the n=100 floor once you fold in the general ±8x scatter; if the volume-decline conjunct is actually running at, say, 0.05-0.10 instead of 0.25-0.35 (plausible under the "lower" reading), the corrected fire-rate estimate could land at 20-50 fires — an inconclusive-starved outcome in the shape of `slot_scarcity_conviction_gate` (166x) and `weekly_pullback_limit_into_uptrend` (186x), both of which "cleared every arithmetic sanity check on paper" and still missed by two orders of magnitude.
- **Lineage:** the distinction from the 3-for-3-dead cross_domain point-process family (Omori, R0, Hawkes — all chased visible acceleration) is real and well-argued; this enters on depletion (volume decline), not acceleration, and aggregates to daily bars specifically to escape the 5-min impulse-chasing failure mode. No objection here.
- **Ceiling/horizon:** 96h hold, tp10/sl5 — comfortably inside the admissible multi-day band, no conflict with the measured sub-12h-empty ceiling.

## 2. `range_compression_then_directional_expansion_gap` — **SKIP**

**Lesson violated:** *MICROSTRUCTURE-FROM-OHLCV LENS EXHAUSTED (r001-r004, 4-for-4 dead)* — "The lens needs order-book/footprint data to proceed; OHLCV candle geometry alone is exhausted."

- The spec's own framing acknowledges the lens is 4-for-4 dead and argues this idea escapes because it reads a "two-phase structural transition" (TR compression → TR expansion with close-position) rather than a single bar's geometry. But the underlying data source is unchanged: true range percentiles and close-position-within-bar are still pure OHLCV-derived candle geometry, computed from the exact same 5-min bars every dead microstructure idea used. The registry's closure condition is explicit and data-source-based, not shape-based ("needs order-book/footprint data to proceed") — a new *sequence* of OHLCV features is not a new *data source*, and the lesson was written precisely to rule out exactly this kind of "same inputs, cleverer geometry" reattempt.
- **Independent, compounding problem — ceiling conflict:** *THE CEILING IS MEASURED* lesson states the sub-12h OHLCV search space is empty below the 0.6% cost floor (gross ceiling only +0.02% to +0.2%/trade for 1h-12h horizons). This idea's mechanism is a same-bar reaction (compression run → decisive expansion bar → enter at that bar's close) with tp5/sl3 over a 24h time exit; the thesis resolves on the immediate expansion bar, not over a multi-day drift — structurally the same sub-12h-resolution shape as the four dead microstructure ideas, all of which lived in exactly this measured-empty territory. Even a well-populated conjunction (which this one plausibly is, given TR autocorrelation) cannot escape a ceiling that's below the fee floor by construction.
- Two independent, sufficient kill reasons (closed lens + measured-empty horizon band) — this is not a borderline call.

## 3. `month_end_rebalance_flow_directional_persistence` — **SKIP**

**Lesson violated:** *BEHAVIORAL-CALENDAR LENS EXHAUSTED IN BOTH DIRECTIONS (r001-r004, 4-for-4 dead)* — "No remaining calendar/price-level permutation is untested; the lens is closed absent a genuinely new data source (real order-book or open-interest, not an OHLCV-derived time or price artifact)."

- The mechanism narrative invokes institutional rebalance flow, but nothing in the entry rule touches a new data source — it is a UTC calendar boundary (an OHLCV-timestamp artifact, same species as the four dead named-time coils) conjoined with prior-month OHLCV return (an OHLCV-price artifact). The lesson's closure condition is explicit that neither of these counts as new; this idea supplies no order-book, no open-interest, no actual flow measurement, only a story about what "must be" driving a return threshold that is directly observable in the same data the four dead ideas already used.
- **Independent problem — direct regime conflict:** the entry conditions the LONG side on "prior month return > +5%" (continuation of the recent directional move at ~monthly scale). *REGIME IS THE VARIABLE* measured directional monthly momentum at **-12.4%, 0/9 cells** across the *entire* 2024-25 archive (which is this project's entire dataset). Betting on monthly-return continuation is precisely the mechanism that lesson found inverted in this epoch, regardless of the calendar-timing dressing.
- **Compounding tell — in-sample sign fitting:** the entry rule hedges its own directional claim ("enter SHORT if prior month return < -5%... but we take the empirically dominant sign, testing long-only if short arm degenerate"). Deciding the tradable sign *after* looking at which arm works is curve-fitting the sign in the same breath as proposing the test — a self-admission that the mechanism's stated causal story (trim-then-reestablish flow) doesn't actually commit to a predicted sign.
- **Decidability is also thin:** the spec itself calls this "the decidability risk... exactly clears n>=100... TIGHT" (~107 fires from 30.6 months × 5 symbols at an assumed 70% qualification rate). A single-condition monthly-cadence base rate is less fragile than a multi-condition daily one, but three problems stacking (closed lens, regime conflict, thin margin) is not a case for spending a slot to find out.

## 4. `constraint_rejection_pressure_release_engine` — **REDESIGN**

**One change that fixes it:** before implementing, premise-check the actual joint frequency of "a symbol's breakout bar is the current bar AND ≥3-of-5 symbols broke out within the trailing 12 bars" directly against the data — do not trust the correlation-adjusted arithmetic alone.

- **Reopening the gate family is legitimate here, on paper:** *GATE FAMILY... CLOSED* explicitly permits a new spec if it names "a specific, named new mechanism for HOW the gate would carry information an ungated engine's own trade stream does not already contain." This idea's mechanism — cross-sectional synchronization count across the *other* 4 symbols — is genuinely absent from any single engine's own trade stream (unlike the five closed-family specs, all of which conditioned on the *same* engine's own past win/loss outcomes). This is not a relabeled outcome-gate; take the reopening claim at face value.
- **But the shape matches the worst-miss pattern on record, not the base-rate math cited:** *RARE-TOUCH CONJUNCTIONS MISS WORST OF ALL* names exactly this structure — "a PRICE TO TOUCH A SPECIFIC LEVEL WITHIN A WINDOW while a slower regime condition also held" — and both instances (`slot_scarcity_conviction_gate`, 166x under; `weekly_pullback_limit_into_uptrend`, 186x under) "cleared every arithmetic sanity check on paper" before missing by two orders of magnitude. This idea's entry rule requires precisely that shape: a specific-bar event (this symbol's breakout is the *current* bar — a touch-like timing condition) nested inside a slower, independently-qualifying regime condition (S≥3 synchronization over a trailing 12-bar window). The idea's fire-rate math (0.12 raw × 0.30 sync-fraction ≈ 0.036/sym/day, ~167 fires) treats the "freshest bar" requirement as a free multiplier rather than modeling how rarely the specific symbol's *own* breakout bar coincides with the window in which the sync condition is *already* true elsewhere — exactly the joint-timing detail the two historical 166x/186x misses got wrong despite sound-looking independent-conjunct arithmetic.
- The prescribed lesson response is explicit: "Premise-check actual touch frequency against the target regime (not just the regime's own frequency) before spending a gauntlet slot on any future 'price comes to a level, inside a multi-day regime' design." That check is cheap (count actual co-occurrences in the historical 5-symbol bar data) and should gate whether this spends a full 930-day slot.
- **Kill criterion is well-formed** (armed win rate vs. same engine's unconditioned win rate, matching the exact retirement clause that closed the gate family) — no complaint there once the premise check is done.

## 5. `multiweek_directional_regime_persistence_hold` — **IMPLEMENT**

No fatal flaw found. This is the round's mandated continuation canary (per *CANARY MANDATE*, 2026-07-20) and is built correctly for that job.

- **Correctly occupies open territory, not closed territory:** *REGIME IS THE VARIABLE* and *REVERSION AT EVERY MEASURED HORIZON* measured the {1,3,6}mo × {1,3,6}mo grid as 0/9 cells for directional momentum in 2024-25 — but that grid never tested a 2-week lookback, and the same lesson set explicitly flags "the 2-week TSMOM crossover point BEAT drift... that weekly-to-monthly band is explicitly flagged OPEN." This idea's 14-day lookback / 7-day hold sits inside that specific open band, not inside the closed monthly territory. It is also correctly time-series directional (own trailing return sign) rather than cross-sectional rank, avoiding the specific trap that killed `trailing_return_rank_persistence_hold` (single-asset momentum in a ranking costume).
- **Correctly required to beat the drift null, not zero:** *SLOW-BAND FIRST MEASUREMENT* established a live drift-null baseline (unconditional weekly buys nets ~fee-floor, ~-0.04%) and mandated that any 2-8-week spec clear that null rather than an absolute positive. The kill_criterion here does exactly that, on both the long and short arm, and explicitly treats a sign-flip as the canary signal itself rather than a failure — the correct design for a canary.
- **Base-rate math is the least fragile in the round:** the fire condition is a *single* univariate threshold (|R14| > 6%) on a return distribution this project can and does measure directly (crypto 2-week moves routinely exceeding 6% is not a speculative conjunction claim, unlike ideas 1, 3, and 4's multi-condition or nested-timing designs). Even the non-overlapping floor estimate (~399 fires) clears n≥100 with room to spare under the ±8x calibration scatter.
- **One non-fatal caveat worth carrying into the gauntlet write-up:** the "evaluate every half-week, hold 7 days" cadence produces heavily overlapping windows (adjacent evaluations share ~75% of their 14-day lookback and much of their 7-day hold). The nominal n (~797) is inflated by serial correlation between adjacent fires on the same symbol; report the non-overlapping/weekly-cadence sample (~399, still decidable) alongside the raw count so the kill-criterion comparison against the drift null isn't reading a pseudo-replicated sample as if it were 797 independent trials.
- **Ceiling:** 168h hold, tp15/sl7 — cost ratio ~4% of target, the best in the round, squarely in the admissible multi-day band.
