**Round thesis.** This round abandons every exhausted entry geometry (candle-shape microstructure, calendar coils, point-process imports, sign-entropy) and every conjunctive gate that starved four consecutive samples. The unifying move is to stop asking candles for direction and instead exploit two structural facts the record has NOT yet tested: (1) the self-referential gate's power came from a SINGLE observable — random entries failing — so this round defines every meta-condition as a single scalar threshold, never a conjunction, deliberately over-firing so armed samples clear n>=100; and (2) the only measured-open OHLCV band is 24h+ maker-friendly holds and 2-7 day persistence, so three of five ideas relocate entirely to the multi-day regime where the fee floor is a rounding error and where the question shifts from 'which direction does this bar resolve' to 'is this symbol in a persistent trending state that will still be trending days from now.' No idea here enters ON an impulse; they enter on the PERSISTENCE of a state, which resolves slower than the impulse and outruns the cost floor by amortization rather than amplitude-per-bar.

## 1. `placebo_losing_streak_single_gate_trend`  ·  lens: meta_self_referential

**Mechanism.** The four dead gate ideas all failed for ONE reason the r004 epitaph named explicitly: the gate required MULTIPLE system_state conditions simultaneously, which is intrinsically rare (n=39, n=7). But the underlying signal the gate detects is real and single-dimensional: when random/placebo entries are currently getting stopped out in a cluster, the market is in a directional (non-chop) regime, because a directional tape stops out entries placed against the trend and lets entries with the trend run. This idea reduces the gate to ONE scalar: the trailing hit-rate of the null_baseline arm over its last N resolved trades. When that single number drops below a threshold (placebo is losing = tape is directional), we arm a simple directional engine that goes WITH the dominant sign of recent bars. No conjunction, no multi-condition state vector. It is not arbitraged because it conditions on the trading SYSTEM'S OWN realized outcomes — a data source no external actor observes — and it deliberately trades the low-information default direction (recent trend sign) only during the windows the self-referential meter says trend is paying.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: trend_persistence_regime_gated_engine (r004) and drawdown_regime_contrarian_gate (r001). This is NOT a descendant because those defined the gate as a CONJUNCTION of system_state conditions (null-arm win rate AND stop-out cluster AND drawdown), which the r004 epitaph proved is the binding scarcity — every one starved. This uses a SINGLE scalar threshold (trailing placebo hit-rate alone), the explicit prescription from the r004 refutation ('a looser or single-condition gate is required before the family can be tested again'). The engine is also not the r004 momentum-persistence engine nor the r001 breakout — it is a plain dominant-sign continuation entry, kept deliberately dumb so the gate does the discriminating.

**Entry rule.** Maintain a rolling window of the last placebo_window=20 resolved null_baseline trades; compute placebo_hitrate = fraction that hit +5% before -3%. GATE OPENS when placebo_hitrate <= placebo_floor=0.30 (random entries are failing = directional regime). While gate is open, on each new 5-min bar compute dominant_sign = sign of the sum of the last trend_lookback=12 bar returns; ENTER LONG if dominant_sign>0 and the current bar closes above the high of the prior bar (with-trend confirmation), ENTER SHORT if dominant_sign<0 and current bar closes below prior bar low. One fire per symbol per cooldown. Direction follows the established trend; the gate ensures we only take it when the self-meter says trend pays.

**Params.** `{"placebo_window": 20, "placebo_floor": 0.3, "trend_lookback": 12, "tp": 0.05, "sl": 0.03, "time_exit_hours": 24}`

**Fire rate.** Base rate arithmetic: the with-trend confirmation (bar closes beyond prior bar's high/low in the dominant direction) fires on ~25% of bars = 72 candidates/sym/day raw. The SINGLE gate (placebo_hitrate<=0.30) is the only filter. Placebo hit-rate against +5/-3 has a base ~38%, and with a 20-trade window its trailing estimate sits below 0.30 roughly 25-30% of the time (a single threshold on one smooth statistic, NOT a rare conjunction — this is the entire point). So armed candidates ~72*0.27 = ~19/sym/day, collapsed by the 1h cooldown to at most ~12 fires/sym/day, further cut by only firing on the first qualifying bar per open-gate episode: conservatively 0.3 fires/sym/day. Over 930d * 5 symbols = 0.3*930*5 = ~1,395 placed. Even if the gate is 5x rarer than estimated, n >= 279. Decidable.

**Fee survival.** This is a 24h thesis at the measured ceiling (gross ~+0.6%, ~+0.25% net maker). It survives ONLY if the single-condition gate concentrates the directional edge the four conjunctive gates couldn't sample: the r001 gate hit 43.3% wins against the asymmetric exit while gross-positive, so a with-trend engine armed by the same meter should push win rate past the ~40% breakeven, and 40%*5% - 60%*3% = +0.2% gross before the gate concentration. The bet is the gate lifts realized win-rate to ~44%, giving +0.4% gross and clearing the floor on maker fills.

**Kill criterion.** Killed if armed win rate <= 40% over n>=100 (matching the asymmetric-exit breakeven), OR if net expectancy < +0.2%/trade. If the single-condition gate ALSO fails to lift win rate above the conjunctive gates' 25-43% band, the entire self-referential family is dead not just its conjunctive form.

## 2. `trailing_return_rank_persistence_hold`  ·  lens: multi_day_horizon

**Mechanism.** Published cross-sectional momentum persistence (the one literature band the prompt flags as open: '1-8 week holds, published persistence, amortizes the floor') says the strongest-trending asset over a multi-week lookback continues to outperform over the next multi-week window. At 5 symbols this is a within-basket relative-strength bet, not a single-bar direction call. The mechanism is slow institutional/allocator rebalancing and reflexive flow-following that operates on weekly, not 5-minute, clocks — precisely why it is invisible to and unarbitraged by 5-min retail: the edge lives at a horizon retail bots structurally cannot hold through (funding, attention span, drawdown tolerance). We enter the top-ranked symbol by trailing multi-day return, hold for days, and let a wide tp/sl and a 120h+ time exit amortize the fixed 0.6% floor across an 8-15% expected move.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: post_shock_multiday_drift (r003). That entered ON a shock bar and found shock direction carries no 5-day signal (71% hit the stop first) — it was a shock-timing entry wearing a multi-day costume. This is fundamentally different: no shock, no impulse entry. It enters on a SLOW-MOVING RANK (trailing multi-day cumulative return leadership), a state that by construction persists (it took days to build), targeting continuation of an established relative-strength regime, not the resolution of a single event. HORIZON IS A LEVER only multiplies an existing edge — and cross-sectional persistence is the one edge the external literature survey says is published and unmeasured here.

**Entry rule.** Every day (once per symbol per day, checked on the 00:00 UTC bar), compute each symbol's cumulative return over rank_lookback_hours=168 (7 days). ENTER LONG on a symbol if it is the TOP-ranked of the 5 by this 7-day return AND its 7-day return > min_leader_return=0.10 (leader must be genuinely trending, not just least-bad). Enter SHORT on the BOTTOM-ranked symbol if its 7-day return < -min_leader_return (genuine downtrend leader). This is a relative-strength continuation; the wide multi-day exit lets the persistence play out.

**Params.** `{"rank_lookback_hours": 168, "min_leader_return": 0.1, "tp": 0.15, "sl": 0.06, "time_exit_hours": 168}`

**Fire rate.** Arithmetic: one evaluation per day. The top symbol always exists; the constraint is min_leader_return>0.10 over 7 days, true for the leader on roughly 55-65% of days (crypto leaders routinely run +10% weekly in trending phases, less in chop). Symmetric short arm on the bottom symbol adds another ~40% of days. So ~1 qualifying fire per day across the basket on the long arm plus ~0.7 on the short, but 1h/symbol cooldown and once-per-day eval mean ~1.0-1.5 placed fires/day total for the basket. Over 930 days = ~930-1,395 placed. Even at a conservative 50% qualification, n >= 465. Decidable and well-powered.

**Fee survival.** The fee floor is 0.6% round-trip; the tp is 15% and sl is 6%. Cost ratio is ~0.04 of the target move — a rounding error, exactly the multi-day lens mandate. Even a marginal 40% win rate on a +15%/-6% payoff yields 0.40*15 - 0.60*6 = +2.4% gross per trade, ~+1.8% net. The edge does not need to be large per-signal; it needs only to be real, because amplitude per fire dwarfs the fixed cost.

**Kill criterion.** Killed if win rate (tp-before-sl) <= 33% over n>=100 (the +15/-6 breakeven is ~29%, so 33% leaves no margin after fees), OR if net expectancy < +0.5%/trade. If cross-sectional leadership shows NO continuation edge at 7-day horizon, the published-persistence prior is refuted for this basket.

## 3. `weekly_pullback_limit_into_uptrend`  ·  lens: cost_engineering

**Mechanism.** The r003 cost-engineering death (pullback_to_breakout_level_limit) proved that intraday retests select FAILING breakouts — the retest within 2h is the failure signal. But that adverse selection is a SHORT-HORIZON artifact: over multi-day windows, a pullback within an established weekly uptrend is the OPPOSITE of failure — it is the normal breathing of a persistent trend, and a limit order placed BELOW current price during that pullback both (a) fills as a maker at a genuine discount and (b) enters an uptrend at a cost basis lower than the trend's mean. The key inversion vs r003: we require the trend to be intact at WEEKLY scale (so the pullback is breathing, not breaking) and we place the entry as a resting limit that price comes to, halving the entry cost. Not arbitraged because holding through a multi-day pullback requires drawdown tolerance retail bots on 5-slot books lack.

**Lineage check.** Nearest death: pullback_to_breakout_level_limit (r003, worst Sharpe ever, 30.9% win). That waited at an INTRADAY broken level within 2h — selecting failing breakouts. This differs on two axes: (1) the trend context is WEEKLY (7-day uptrend of >8%), so the pullback is trend-breathing not breakout-failure, and (2) the hold is multi-day with a wide exit, so an intraday adverse-selection artifact cannot dominate a days-long continuation. r003 proved cost-engineering can't fix adverse selection in a failing-signal entry; this removes the adverse selection by changing horizon, then applies the maker discount to a genuine trend-continuation.

**Entry rule.** Identify symbols in a weekly uptrend: 7-day (168h) return > uptrend_min=0.08 AND current price above the 200-bar (16.7h) close-mean. When such a symbol pulls back to pullback_depth=0.03 (3%) below its trailing 24h high, place a resting LIMIT BUY at that level (price comes to you = maker fill). Fill only if touched within limit_life_hours=12; else cancel. One fire per symbol per cooldown. Long-only continuation into an established weekly uptrend at a discounted maker entry.

**Params.** `{"uptrend_min": 0.08, "pullback_depth": 0.03, "limit_life_hours": 12, "tp": 0.12, "sl": 0.05, "time_exit_hours": 120}`

**Fire rate.** Arithmetic: at any time ~1.5 of 5 symbols are in a >8% weekly uptrend (crypto trends broadly correlated; ~30% of symbol-days). Within an uptrend, a 3%-below-24h-high pullback that touches the limit occurs on ~40% of uptrend days (normal intraday breathing routinely reaches 3% dips). So placed fires ~ 5 symbols * 0.30 uptrend * 0.40 touch = ~0.6 fills/day for the basket, cooldown-limited but eval'd continuously. Over 930 days = ~558 placed. Conservative halving still gives n >= 279. Decidable.

**Fee survival.** Maker entry on the limit fill cuts round-trip cost from ~0.6% toward ~0.35% (maker-side savings). tp is 12%, sl 5% — cost is ~0.03 of target. The discounted entry (3% below the 24h high, in a still-rising trend) also improves the effective payoff geometry: entering a +8%/week trend 3% cheaper raises the odds of the 12% target before the 5% stop. Breakeven win rate on +12/-5 is ~29%; a real weekly uptrend should continue past 12% well over a third of the time.

**Kill criterion.** Killed if maker limit-fill win rate (tp-before-sl) <= 33% over n>=100, OR net expectancy < +0.5%/trade. If pullbacks-in-weekly-uptrends select failures the way intraday retests did (r003), win rate collapses below 33% and the horizon-fix is refuted.

## 5. `multiday_magnitude_persistence_directional_hold`  ·  lens: information_theory

**Mechanism.** Both prior information-theory deaths failed the SAME way: they detected regime BIRTH (entropy break, magnitude surprise) but regime birth doesn't tell you DIRECTION (r004: 'compressibility break identifies volatility-regime birth; it does not identify which direction'). The fix is to stop asking the info-theoretic signal for direction and use it only as a REGIME-QUALITY filter, taking direction from the established trend. Specifically: measure the compressibility (via realized-magnitude autocorrelation) of a symbol's DAILY return sequence over a multi-week window — volatility CLUSTERS, so magnitude is persistent (this is the one thing measured to have headroom: r004 confirmed p99 surprise 4.6). When magnitude persistence is HIGH (volatility is clustering = a real regime is running, not noise), the directional trend within that regime is more likely to persist too, because trends and vol-clusters co-occur in crypto. We hold multi-day in the trend's direction only when the magnitude-persistence meter says a genuine regime (not chop) is active. Unarbitraged because it operates on daily-bar magnitude structure over weeks, invisible to 5-min bots.

**Lineage check.** Nearest deaths: return_magnitude_compressibility_break (r004) and conditional_entropy_regime_expansion (r002). Both used the info signal to pick DIRECTION at 5-min scale and both were direction-blind. This uses the info signal ONLY as a regime-quality gate (is volatility genuinely clustering?) and takes direction from the multi-day trend — never asking entropy/magnitude for a sign. It also moves to DAILY bars over a multi-week window (r002's explicit prescription: 'if information theory returns it must use magnitudes or longer bars, not signs') and a multi-day hold, escaping the 5-min-sign regime where the lens died twice.

**Entry rule.** For each symbol, on the daily 00:00 UTC bar, build the sequence of the last mag_window=21 daily absolute returns. Compute mag_persistence = lag-1 autocorrelation of that |return| sequence. QUALIFY the symbol if mag_persistence >= persistence_min=0.25 (volatility is genuinely clustering = live regime). Among qualifying symbols, take DIRECTION from the trend: 168h return sign. ENTER LONG if qualified and 168h return > regime_trend_min=0.06; ENTER SHORT if qualified and 168h return < -0.06. Multi-day hold. Info-theory picks the regime; trend picks the side.

**Params.** `{"mag_window": 21, "persistence_min": 0.25, "regime_trend_min": 0.06, "tp": 0.14, "sl": 0.05, "time_exit_hours": 144}`

**Fire rate.** Arithmetic: daily eval, 5 symbols. mag_persistence >= 0.25 on daily |returns| is common in crypto (vol clustering is strong; the lag-1 autocorr of daily |returns| is typically 0.2-0.4), true for ~50% of symbol-days. The trend condition (|168h return|>0.06) is true ~55% of symbol-days. These are POSITIVELY correlated (clustering vol and trending price co-occur), so the conjunction is NOT multiplicative-small — expect ~35% of symbol-days qualify (stated explicitly per the conjunction-correlation lesson: I am NOT multiplying 0.5*0.55=0.275 as independent; positive correlation keeps it ~0.35). So ~5*0.35 = 1.75 qualifying/day, cooldown/once-per-day capped at ~1.2 placed/day. Over 930 days = ~1,116 placed. Even at 30% of estimate, n >= 334. Decidable.

**Fee survival.** Multi-day 144h hold, tp 14% / sl 5%, cost ~0.04 of target — floor amortized away. The magnitude-persistence filter is designed to raise the QUALITY of the regime (live vol-cluster = trend more likely to run to the 14% target before the 5% stop), lifting win rate above the +14/-5 breakeven of ~26%. A genuine clustering regime should carry a trending symbol to 14% well over a third of the time.

**Kill criterion.** Killed if win rate (tp-before-sl) <= 33% over n>=100, OR net expectancy < +0.5%/trade, OR if qualifying-vs-non-qualifying symbols show no win-rate difference (magnitude persistence adds no regime-quality information). The pre-implementation premise check must confirm lag-1 |return| autocorrelation actually reaches 0.25+ on ~40%+ of symbol-days before spending the full gauntlet.
